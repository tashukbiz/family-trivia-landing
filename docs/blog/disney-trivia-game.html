Disney Trivia Game: How to Play With Kids on One Phone

A practical guide to running a Disney trivia game with kids, whether you use paper, cards, or a shared device.

If your kids love Disney, a trivia game is an easy win for family time. You can keep it quick, play with mixed ages, and use questions your kids already know from favorite movies. For a big set of questions, start with the Disney trivia questions hub and then use the tips below to turn them into a game.

Different Ways Families Play Disney Trivia

  • Quick round in the car using oral questions.
  • Family game night with a score sheet.
  • Team play with siblings vs. parents.
  • Shared-device trivia on one phone.

Pros and Cons of Paper, Cards, Videos, and Apps

Paper or Index Cards

Great for customizing, but they take time to prepare and can be hard to reuse on the go.

Trivia Videos

Fun visuals, but they move at someone else’s pace and don’t always match your kids’ age levels.

Board or Card Games

Great for family nights, but not always portable or tailored to Disney themes.

Trivia Apps

Easy to start, portable, and can adjust difficulty for each player.

Why Shared-Device Trivia Works Well for Families

One phone keeps the focus on conversation rather than screens. Kids take turns, parents can help with reading, and everyone stays engaged. It’s especially helpful when siblings are at different reading levels or have different Disney favorites.

How to Play as a Family Game

Use a short round of 10–12 questions. Let each player choose a category or movie, and give one point per correct answer. Keep it light and celebrate good guesses.

1. In Moana, what is the name of the demigod who helps Moana?

Answer: Maui

2. What is the name of the cowboy toy in Toy Story?

Answer: Woody

3. In Frozen, what is the name of Elsa’s sister?

Answer: Anna

4. What kind of animal is Simba?

Answer: A lion

5. In Encanto, who is the super-strong sister?

Answer: Luisa

6. What is the name of the fish in Finding Nemo?

Answer: Nemo

How to Start a Disney Trivia Game in Minutes

  1. Pick a short round length (10–15 minutes).
  2. Choose easy questions for younger kids.
  3. Rotate turns so every player participates.
  4. End with a fun prize like choosing the next movie.

Disney Trivia Game FAQs

How long should a Disney trivia game last?

A 10–15 minute round is perfect for kids. It feels fun and fast without losing attention.

What’s the easiest way to play without prep?

Use a shared device game so each player gets a turn with questions suited to their age.

How do I keep a Disney trivia game fair for siblings?

Mix easy and medium questions, and rotate turns. Give younger kids a hint after a few seconds.

Do I need printed cards or a game board?

No. A list of questions or a simple phone-based game is enough for a full family round.
