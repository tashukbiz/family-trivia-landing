How to Use the Family Trivia App (Step by Step)

This guide explains exactly how Family Trivia works on one shared device and gives you the best 10 topics to start with.

Quick Answer

Family Trivia is a local multiplayer trivia app for 1 to 10 players on a single device. One person sets up the game, players take turns answering, and the app clearly shows whose turn it is and which round you are on.

You can run 3 to 15 rounds, and each player can choose their own name, topic, and difficulty (Easy, Medium, Hard).

How to Play Family Trivia in 6 Steps

1) Start Local Multiplayer on One Device

Open the app and choose your local game. Family Trivia is designed for shared-device gameplay, so everyone plays on one phone or tablet.

2) Add Players or Teams (1-10)

Enter each player/team name. You can play solo, as a couple, or with a full group of up to 10.

3) Pick Round Count (3-15)

Choose how long you want the session to be. For first-time groups, 5 to 8 rounds is a sweet spot.

4) Customize Each Player

Every player (or team) can select a trivia topic and difficulty level independently, so beginners and experts can both enjoy the same game.

5) Pass the Device Each Turn

The facilitator sets the flow, but the app makes turns clear so the device can pass smoothly from player to player.

6) Track Progress by Round

During play, everyone sees the current round and total rounds, making pacing easier for road trips, family nights, and casual gatherings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many people can play Family Trivia on one device?

Family Trivia supports 1 to 10 players or teams in local multiplayer on a single shared device.

How many rounds should we choose?

You can set between 3 and 15 rounds. Most families start with 5 to 8 rounds for a quick, fun session.

Can each player choose different topics and difficulty?

Yes. Each player or team can pick their own name, trivia topic, and difficulty level (Easy, Medium, Hard).

Is Family Trivia online multiplayer?

No. Family Trivia is designed for shared-device play, where people pass one phone or tablet between turns.
